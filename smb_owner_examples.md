# SMB Owner Examples for Fine-Tuning

Real-world small business descriptions written from the perspective of non-technical owners who would approach the CustomERP platform to manage their operations. Each entry captures how a real person would describe their business and what they need -- in their own words, without ERP jargon.

These examples intentionally include requests that go beyond the current generator's capabilities (marked with `*` where the need exceeds supported scope) to train the AI to respond honestly about limitations while still being helpful.

---

## 1. Neighborhood Auto Repair Shop

I run a small auto repair garage with 6 mechanics. We fix cars, trucks, and sometimes motorcycles. I need to track my employees, their shifts, and overtime. We buy parts from different suppliers -- brake pads, oil filters, tires, belts -- and I need to know what's in stock so I don't order stuff I already have. When a customer brings in a car, I want to create a work order, list what parts we used, how many labor hours, and then generate an invoice. Right now I do all this on paper and it's a mess. I also want customers to be able to see their invoice history if they call back. Some parts have warranties and I need to track expiry on things like fluids and adhesives.

## 2. Family-Owned Bakery

We're a family bakery, been around 15 years. Me, my wife, and 4 employees. We bake bread, pastries, cakes, and custom wedding cakes. I need something to track my ingredients -- flour, sugar, butter, eggs -- because I'm always running out of something at the worst time. I want low stock alerts on my phone* when we're running low. We also do custom cake orders and I need to invoice customers with deposits upfront and the rest on pickup. My wife handles payroll and she needs to track everyone's hours and calculate pay. We sell wholesale to two local cafes so I need separate pricing for wholesale vs retail customers. I'd also love a simple point-of-sale* thing but I know that might be too much.

---

## 3. Plumbing & Heating Services

I'm a plumber, I have a small company with 8 technicians and 2 office staff. We do residential and commercial plumbing, boiler installs, and emergency callouts. I need to schedule jobs and assign them to technicians*. When a job is done, the technician should be able to log what parts they used -- pipes, fittings, valves, sealant -- and how many hours the job took. Then I need to send the customer an invoice, sometimes with payment terms of 30 days for commercial clients. I also need to track my van stock -- each technician has a van with common parts and I need to know what's in each van. For employees, I need to track certifications and when they expire because gas safety certs are legally required.

---

## 4. Boutique Clothing Store

I own a small women's clothing boutique. I have 3 staff including myself. I buy clothing from about 10 different designers and suppliers. I need to track my inventory by size and color -- like I might have the same dress in 4 sizes and 3 colors, each needs its own stock count. I want to know which items are selling well and which are just sitting there*. When items come in, I need to log them, and when they sell, the stock should go down. I invoice wholesale customers who buy for their own shops, and I need to track who owes me money. For my staff, just basic hours and pay is fine. I'd also like to set up seasonal sales with discounted pricing* on older stock.

## 5. Small Construction Contractor

I run a construction company, about 20 people -- carpenters, masons, laborers, and 3 in the office. We do residential renovations, extensions, and small commercial fitouts. I need to track materials by job site -- cement, timber, steel, fixtures -- and know what's allocated where. Invoicing is project-based: I give a quote, then invoice in stages as milestones are completed. I need progress billing -- like 30% upfront, 30% at framing, 40% at completion. For my crew, I need to track which site they're on each day, their hours, and different pay rates for overtime and weekends. I also need to track subcontractors and what I owe them*. Safety certifications for workers need tracking too.

## 6. Pet Grooming Salon

I have a dog and cat grooming salon with 4 groomers plus a receptionist. We do baths, haircuts, nail trimming, teeth cleaning, and specialty treatments. I need to keep track of my appointments* and customer pets -- like which dog gets what cut, any allergies or special notes. I buy grooming supplies in bulk -- shampoos, conditioners, clippers, blades -- and I need to know when to reorder. I invoice customers after each visit and some of my regulars have monthly packages that I bill once a month. For my groomers, I track their hours and some of them earn commission on upsells* like premium shampoo treatments. I'd also love to send appointment reminders to customers*.

## 7. Small Accounting Firm

I'm a CPA with a small practice -- myself and 5 accountants plus 2 admin staff. We handle bookkeeping, tax returns, payroll, and advisory for about 200 small business clients. I need to track billable hours per client and generate invoices based on time spent. Different staff have different hourly rates. I need to track client accounts receivable -- who's paid, who hasn't, aging reports*. For my team, I need leave management, timesheets, and performance tracking. We don't have physical inventory but I do buy office supplies and need to track expenses*. I want to be able to see profitability per client* -- how much we bill vs how much time we spend.

## 8. Local Gym / Fitness Studio

I run a gym with about 12 staff -- personal trainers, class instructors, front desk, and a cleaner. We have memberships (monthly, annual, and pay-as-you-go) and I need to invoice members and track payments. Some members pay by direct debit* and some pay cash. I sell supplements, protein bars, gym gear, and water bottles so I need inventory tracking for the retail side. For staff, I need to track schedules, hours, and the trainers get paid differently based on how many sessions they do. I'd also love member check-in tracking* and usage stats but I don't know if that's possible. Equipment maintenance tracking* would be nice too -- knowing when machines need servicing.

## 9. Catering Company

We're a catering business, 8 full-time staff plus about 15 freelance servers and chefs we call for events. We do weddings, corporate events, birthday parties, and weekly meal prep for offices. I need to manage event bookings* -- date, venue, menu, headcount, and then generate invoices with deposits. We buy ingredients in bulk and I need to track perishables carefully -- expiry dates on everything. Some ingredients need to be ordered special for specific events and I need to plan ahead. For my staff, the full-timers get salaries and the freelancers get paid per event. I need to track who worked which event and how many hours. I'd also like to track my recipes* and auto-calculate ingredient quantities based on headcount.

## 10. Dental Clinic

I'm a dentist with a small practice -- 2 dentists, 3 dental hygienists, 2 nurses, and a receptionist. We do checkups, cleanings, fillings, crowns, implants, and orthodontics. I need to manage patient appointments* and records. For billing, we invoice patients or their insurance companies, sometimes splitting a bill between the patient and their insurance. I need to track dental supplies -- composite filling material, anesthetics, disposable gloves, sterilization supplies -- and many of these have batch numbers and expiry dates. For staff, I need to track hours, holidays, and continuing education requirements. I also need to be HIPAA compliant* in how I handle patient data.

## 11. Print Shop

I run a printing business with 5 employees. We do business cards, flyers, banners, t-shirt printing, and large format signage. Customers come in or email orders. I need to track my materials -- different paper stocks, inks, vinyl rolls, blank t-shirts by size and color. Each job uses different quantities of materials and I need to deduct from stock when a job is completed. I invoice customers per job, sometimes with rush charges for fast turnaround. Repeat customers get volume discounts. For my team, it's straightforward -- track hours and pay. I'd also like to track job status* -- received, in production, quality check, ready for pickup -- so I can tell customers where their order is.

## 12. Landscaping & Garden Services

I have a landscaping company with 10 workers and 2 office people. We do lawn mowing, garden design, tree trimming, irrigation installation, and seasonal cleanups. I buy supplies -- mulch, topsoil, plants, fertilizer, irrigation parts -- and need to track what's used on each job. We invoice residential customers per visit and commercial customers monthly. Some customers are on recurring maintenance contracts* and I need to auto-generate monthly invoices for them. For my crew, they work in teams of 2-3 and I need to track hours, travel time, and their certifications like pesticide applicator licenses. Equipment tracking -- mowers, trimmers, blowers -- would be great too* so I know what's out and what needs maintenance.

## 13. Independent Pharmacy

We sell prescription medications, over-the-counter drugs, vitamins, personal care products, and some medical devices. Inventory is critical -- I need batch tracking, expiry dates, and low stock alerts on everything, especially controlled substances which have strict regulations*. We invoice patients and insurance companies. Some medications are partially covered by insurance and the patient pays the difference. For staff, I track shifts, hours, and continuing education credits. I'd need integration with prescription management systems* but I know that's probably outside what you do.

## 14. Small Furniture Workshop

My workshop makes custom furniture -- tables, chairs, cabinets, bookshelves. We're 6 people: 4 woodworkers, an apprentice, and me. I buy raw materials -- lumber by species and dimension, hardware, stains, lacquers, upholstery fabric -- and I need to track all of it. When I make a piece, I want to record what materials went into it so I can price it properly. Customers order custom pieces and I invoice them with 50% deposit and 50% on delivery. Some pieces I make for showroom stock and sell later. I need to track my employees' hours and the apprentice has a different pay structure. I'd also love a way to attach photos* to each product or order.

## 15. IT Support & Services Company

We sell computers, networking equipment, and accessories, and also provide support services -- installation, troubleshooting, maintenance contracts. I need to track my hardware inventory with serial numbers -- every laptop, monitor, router I sell needs to be traceable. Service jobs need to be tracked* -- customer reports an issue, we assign a tech, track the time, and then invoice. I bill hourly for ad-hoc support and monthly for contract customers. For my staff, I need to track their hours, skills, and certifications like Microsoft or Cisco certs. I'd also need some kind of ticketing* system but maybe that's separate.

## 16. Food Truck Business

I need to track my ingredients -- meat, buns, tortillas, sauces, vegetables -- all perishable with short shelf lives. Each truck needs its own inventory count because they're stocked independently. I invoice event organizers when we're booked for private events, and for daily sales I just need to record revenue. My employees rotate between trucks and I need to track who worked where and their hours. I'd love GPS tracking on my trucks* but mainly I just need to stop losing money on spoiled food and not knowing my actual costs per item*.

## 17. Real Estate Agency

I don't have physical inventory but I need to track property listings*, client information, and which agent is handling which deal. For invoicing, we bill commission on completed sales. Some deals involve split commissions with other agencies. For my team, I need to track their base salary plus commission calculations*. Admin staff are on regular salaries with standard leave tracking. I'd also need document management* for contracts and agreements, and ideally a way to track leads and follow-ups* -- like a simple CRM.

## 18. Craft Brewery

Our production is in batches* and I need to trace which ingredients went into which batch for quality control. We sell kegs to local pubs and restaurants (invoice with net-30 terms), cans through our taproom (daily sales), and online orders*. For employees, just basic hours, leave, and pay tracking. I'd also need to track excise duty* on production since we pay tax per liter brewed.

I invoice clients per session or per package, usually with a booking deposit and final payment on delivery. Wedding packages can be paid in installments. ## 22. Auto Parts Store

I own an auto parts shop with 4 employees. We sell everything from spark plugs and brake pads to engine oil, batteries, and wiper blades. We have about 3,000 different SKUs. I need proper inventory management -- reorder points, stock counts, barcode scanning*. Some parts are vehicle-specific and I need to be able to look them up by make, model, and year*. We sell to walk-in customers and also to local mechanics who have trade accounts with credit terms. I invoice trade customers weekly or monthly. Returns and warranties need tracking. For staff, just hours and pay. I'd also need integration with parts catalogs* to look up compatibility.

## 23. Daycare Center

Some parents qualify for government subsidies* that cover part of the fee, and I need to track and bill accordingly. For staff, I need to maintain mandated child-to-staff ratios* so I track shifts carefully. I also need to track certifications like first aid and safeguarding training expiry. When a customer orders, I create a quote first, then convert to an invoice after they approve. Big orders get delivered and I charge a delivery fee. Installation jobs are quoted separately and invoiced on completion. For my team, the installers work on-site and I need to track their hours per job. ## 35. Small Machine Shop

We do custom CNC machining, welding, and metal fabrication for industrial clients. I have 8 machinists, 2 welders, and 2 in the office. I buy raw materials -- steel bar, aluminum plate, stainless sheet, cutting tools, welding wire -- all tracked by dimensions and grade. Each job uses specific materials and I need to track consumption per work order. I quote jobs based on material cost, machine time, and labor, then invoice on completion or in stages for large orders. Cutting tools wear out and I need to track their life cycles. For my team, machinists operate different machines and I track their hours and which jobs they worked on. I'd also need quality inspection tracking* -- measurements, tolerances, pass/fail records that we send to clients.
